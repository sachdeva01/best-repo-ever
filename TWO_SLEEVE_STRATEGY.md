### ⚠️ Inflation Gap — The Key Risk to Manage

With 3% inflation, expenses grow from $275K today to ~$441K by age 67. The income sleeve yield stays roughly fixed in dollar terms unless the underlying assets have **yield growth**. Gap by age 67: ~$67K/year even after Social Security.

**Solution — build yield growth into the income sleeve:**
- SCHD dividend growth: ~8%/year historically
- VIG dividend growth: ~6–7%/year
- Target 35–40% of income sleeve in dividend growers (not just munis)
- Munis provide stability; dividend growers provide inflation protection

A 40% allocation to SCHD/VIG/VYM within the income sleeve means yield grows ~3–4%/year, roughly matching inflation. This closes the gap without ever touching principal.

## Tax Optimization

### Tax-Advantaged Account Usage

**Tax-Free (Muni Bonds):** Use in **taxable accounts**
- VTEB, MUB, HYD, VTES in taxable accounts
- Maximize tax-free income

**Tax-Deferred (Growth Stocks):** Use in **401(k)/IRA accounts**
- VOO, VGT, VXF, VXUS in retirement accounts
- Defer taxes on growth and dividends

**Qualified Dividends (SCHD, JEPI):** Can go in **either**
- Preferably taxable accounts (lower 15-20% rate)
- Better than ordinary income

### Withdrawal Sequencing

**Ages 57-59.5:** (Before penalty-free 401k access)
1. Draw from taxable accounts only
2. Pay attention to capital gains

**Ages 59.5-67:** (Before Social Security)
1. Start drawing from 401(k) accounts as needed
2. Convert some traditional IRA → Roth (if in low tax bracket)

**Ages 67+:** (With Social Security)
1. Social Security covers ~$36K/year
2. Income Sleeve covers rest
3. Let Growth Sleeve compound

## Risk Management

### What Could Go Wrong?

**Market Crash:** Income Sleeve could drop in value
- **Mitigation:** Diversified across munis + dividends
- Keep 2 years expenses in cash outside sleeves

**Interest Rates Rise:** Muni bond prices fall
- **Mitigation:** Ladder maturities (VTES short-term, VTEB intermediate)
- Hold to maturity if needed

**Inflation Higher Than 3%:** Expenses rise faster than income
- **Mitigation:** Some inflation-protected securities (TIPS)
- Growth Sleeve provides cushion
- Social Security is inflation-adjusted

**Dividend Cuts:** SCHD, JEPI reduce distributions
- **Mitigation:** Diversified income sources
- Can tap Growth Sleeve temporarily

### Emergency Procedures

**If Income Sleeve income drops below $250K:**
1. Review causes (market, dividend cuts, etc.)
2. Move $1M from Growth → Income Sleeve
3. Rebalance Income Sleeve to restore 4% yield

**If both sleeves drop 30%+:**
1. Reduce expenses by 20% temporarily
2. Delay major purchases
3. Let portfolio recover before major rebalancing
