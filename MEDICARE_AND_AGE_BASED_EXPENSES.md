## Detailed Medicare Planning

### What Medicare Covers (Age 65+)

#### Medicare Part A (Hospital Insurance)
- **Cost:** Free (if you paid Medicare taxes for 10+ years)
- **Covers:** Hospital stays, skilled nursing, hospice

#### Medicare Part B (Medical Insurance)
- **Cost:** $174/month in 2024 ($2,088/year)
- **Covers:** Doctor visits, outpatient care, preventive services
- **Note:** Premium adjusted based on income (IRMAA)

#### Medicare Part D (Prescription Drug Coverage)
- **Cost:** ~$50/month average ($600/year)
- **Covers:** Prescription medications
- **Note:** Shop plans annually during open enrollment

#### Medigap or Medicare Advantage
- **Medigap Plan G:** ~$200/month ($2,400/year)
  - Covers most out-of-pocket costs
  - Best for frequent medical needs
- **Medicare Advantage:** $0-100/month
  - Replaces Part A, B, D
  - Often includes extras (dental, vision, hearing)
  - More restrictive networks

**Recommendation:** Budget $5,000/year for Medicare supplemental coverage.

## What Happens to Pre-Medicare Health Insurance

### Ages 51-64: Pre-Medicare Coverage Options

Since you're retiring at 54, you'll need health insurance for **11 years** until Medicare starts.

#### Option 1: ACA Marketplace (Healthcare.gov)
- **Cost:** $1,500-3,000/month for family ($18K-36K/year)
- **With subsidies:** Potentially lower if MAGI is under $100K
- **Pros:** Comprehensive coverage, nationwide access
- **Cons:** High premiums without subsidies

#### Option 2: COBRA (from employer)
- **Cost:** ~100% of employer plan + 2% admin fee
- **Duration:** Up to 18-36 months maximum
- **Pros:** Keep current coverage temporarily
- **Cons:** Very expensive, time-limited

#### Option 3: Health Sharing Ministry
- **Cost:** $500-800/month ($6K-10K/year)
- **Pros:** Much cheaper than traditional insurance
- **Cons:** Not true insurance, may not cover everything

#### Option 4: High-Deductible Plan + HSA
- **Cost:** $1,000-1,500/month ($12K-18K/year)
- **Pros:** Lower premiums, HSA tax benefits
- **Cons:** High out-of-pocket costs

**Current Budget: $26,000/year**
This covers a solid ACA plan or COBRA for ~2 years, then ACA.

### Medicare IRMAA (Income-Related Monthly Adjustment Amount)

If your Modified Adjusted Gross Income (MAGI) exceeds thresholds, Medicare Part B and D premiums increase.

**2024 IRMAA Thresholds (per person):**
| MAGI | Part B Monthly | Part D Monthly |
|------|----------------|----------------|
| <$103K | $174 | $0 |
| $103K-129K | $244 | $12.90 |
| $129K-161K | $349 | $33.30 |
| $161K-193K | $454 | $53.80 |
| $193K-500K | $559 | $74.20 |
| >$500K | $594 | $81.00 |

**Your situation:**
- **MAGI:** ~$276K net income (likely closer to $200K MAGI after deductions)
- **IRMAA Bracket:** Likely $193K-500K
- **Extra Cost:** ~$385/month = $4,620/year for Part B
- **Part D surcharge:** ~$74/month = $888/year

**Total Medicare with IRMAA: ~$7,600/year** (vs. $5,000 budgeted)

**Action:** Budget an extra $2,600/year for IRMAA surcharges, or plan to reduce MAGI through tax strategies (Roth conversions, charitable giving, etc.).
